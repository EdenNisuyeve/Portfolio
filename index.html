I recently graduated with a BA in Instructional Technologies from the Holon Institute of Technology (HIT), where I studied design, development, and learning. I enjoy thinking about how small details come together to create a better overall experience.

After finishing my degree, my partner and I decided to pause everything and travel around Southeast Asia for a few months.
Even while traveling, I couldn't help but notice small UX moments everywhere.

See some examples
Toilet paper without a center roll sounds smart since it gives you more paper, but I kept dropping it while trying to use it — which actually made me use more 😅. Good intention, poor affordance.
Sinks where the faucet is too close to the edge, so instead of washing my hands, I ended up hitting the sink — which wasn't always very clean. A tiny spatial decision with real consequences.
